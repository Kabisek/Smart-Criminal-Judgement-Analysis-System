My name is Kumar. I operate a sizable hardware establishment in Jaffna. For the preceding five years, an individual identified as Rajan was employed as an accountant at my establishment. He was exclusively responsible for managing all financial accounts pertaining to the store's revenue and expenditure, as well as all banking transactions. During the annual audit of our establishment conducted last month, a significant financial irregularity was uncovered in the accounts. Without my knowledge or authorization, Rajan fabricated fraudulent invoices in the names of non-existent entities and subsequently misappropriated approximately 50 lakh rupees from the store's financial reserves. Furthermore, he forged my signature on my chequebook and illegally withdrew funds from the bank. Upon confronting Rajan regarding these illicit activities, he initially admitted culpability for the offense, exhibiting distress. Nevertheless, he is currently presenting a divergent narrative to law enforcement authorities. He asserts that I, Kumar, the proprietor, instructed him to undertake these actions as part of a tax evasion scheme. He is making false statements, claiming that the misappropriated funds were remitted directly to me. I am in possession of forged documents created by Rajan and corroborating CCTV footage. Despite the evidence, he is now contending his innocence.
